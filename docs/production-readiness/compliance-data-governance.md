# Compliance, Data Residency and Auditability

## Gate
Issue #35 — Compliance, Data Residency and Auditability

## Required inventory

- Data categories processed by SANAD.
- Personal, financial, employee, customer, authentication, audit, and operational data.
- Data owner and processing purpose for each category.
- Storage provider, region, retention period, and deletion method.
- Internal and external recipients and processors.
- Cross-border transfer and residency decision.

## Saudi compliance baseline

- Record the lawful processing purpose and accountable owner.
- Apply data minimization and purpose limitation.
- Define retention and secure deletion rules.
- Protect data subject request procedures.
- Document processors and sub-processors.
- Maintain access and administrative audit logs.
- Establish breach detection, assessment, escalation, and notification procedures.
- Review applicable Saudi PDPL and sector-specific obligations before commercial launch.

## Current pilot classification

The current Vercel, Render, and Supabase free-tier environment is approved only for pilot development and integration. It is not approved for unrestricted commercial personal or regulated data processing.

## Commercial production evidence

- Approved data-flow diagram and processing inventory.
- Signed data residency decision.
- Provider region and contractual responsibility record.
- Retention and deletion matrix.
- Audit-log coverage and protection evidence.
- Privacy notice and data-subject procedure review.
- Open compliance gaps assigned with owners and due dates.

## Exit rule

Issue #35 closes only when the data inventory, residency decision, retention controls, auditability evidence, and applicable legal review contain no unresolved production blocker.
