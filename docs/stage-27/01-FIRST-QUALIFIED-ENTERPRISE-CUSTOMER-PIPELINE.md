# Stage 27 — First Qualified Enterprise Customer Pipeline

## Purpose

This document defines the first qualified enterprise customer pipeline for SNAD after Stage 26 closure.

The objective is to create a controlled, measurable, and governance-safe customer acquisition path that identifies qualified enterprise prospects, prioritizes the first pilot candidate, and prepares the first customer acquisition motion without changing production release status.

## Ideal Customer Profile

A qualified enterprise prospect should meet at least five of the following criteria:

- 20+ employees or multi-department operations
- Active need for ERP, CRM, HRM, Accounting, Workflow, or Commerce consolidation
- Manual approval workflows causing measurable delays
- Arabic-first or bilingual operational requirements
- Executive sponsor available
- Pilot budget or clear procurement path
- Ability to provide structured implementation feedback
- Data/security requirements can be reviewed before onboarding
- Partner-led implementation is acceptable

## Qualification Criteria

A prospect is considered qualified only when the following are documented:

1. Business pain
2. Target modules
3. Decision maker
4. Executive sponsor
5. Pilot success criteria
6. Timeline
7. Data/security constraints
8. Commercial path
9. Implementation owner

## Lead Scoring Model

| Signal | Score |
|---|---:|
| Strong operational pain | 20 |
| Executive sponsor present | 15 |
| Multi-module need | 15 |
| Pilot timeline under 60 days | 10 |
| Partner implementation accepted | 10 |
| Legal/commercial path clear | 10 |
| Arabic-first requirement | 10 |
| Expansion potential | 10 |

Scoring bands:

```text
80–100: Priority pilot candidate
60–79: Qualified opportunity
40–59: Nurture / revisit
Below 40: Not ready
```

## Prospect Sources

- Partner referrals
- Existing professional network
- SME communities
- Industry associations
- LinkedIn outreach
- Accounting/ERP pain-based campaigns
- Workflow automation campaigns

## First 10 Prospect Plan

The first Stage 27 execution cycle should create a target list of 10 qualified enterprise prospects with the following fields:

```text
Company name
Industry
Contact owner
Business pain
Target modules
Decision maker
Estimated users
Pilot readiness
Lead score
Next action
```

## First 3 Demo Plan

From the first 10 prospects, select the top 3 for demo execution based on:

- Lead score
- Executive sponsor availability
- Implementation urgency
- Product fit
- Pilot feasibility

## First Pilot Customer Selection

The first pilot customer must have:

- Clear business pain
- Defined scope
- Named sponsor
- Onboarding window
- Approved pilot success criteria
- No unresolved critical data/security blocker

## Acquisition Risks

| Risk | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Weak customer fit | Use ICP and lead scoring |
| Long decision cycle | Prioritize urgent operational pain |
| Legal delay | Use Stage 27 legal/commercial tracker |
| Scope creep | Limit first pilot scope |
| Partner readiness gap | Assign partner only after qualification |
