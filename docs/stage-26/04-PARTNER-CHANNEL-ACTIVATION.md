# Stage 26 — Partner Channel Activation

## Purpose

This document defines the partner channel activation model required to move SNAD from partner readiness into active partner-led market execution.

The goal is to recruit, qualify, enable, and measure partners who can support implementation, integrations, sales referrals, and regional market expansion.

## Partner Types

| Partner Type | Role |
|---|---|
| Implementation Partner | Configure SNAD, onboard customers, deliver training |
| Integration Partner | Connect SNAD with external systems and customer tools |
| Consulting Partner | Advise on process redesign and workflow automation |
| Sales Partner | Source and qualify customer opportunities |
| Marketplace Partner | Build extensions, templates, or industry packages |

## Partner Activation Funnel

```text
Target Partner Identified
Initial Contact
Partner Qualification
Capability Review
Commercial Discussion
Partner Agreement Drafted
Enablement Completed
First Opportunity Assigned
First Customer Delivered
Partner Performance Review
```

## Partner Qualification Criteria

A partner is qualified only if the following are documented:

- Business registration or operating identity
- Implementation capability
- Customer-facing team
- Industry focus
- Technical capability
- Data/security awareness
- Commercial expectations
- Geographic coverage

## Enablement Package

Each partner must receive:

1. SNAD overview
2. Product positioning
3. Demo flow
4. Implementation checklist
5. Security and privacy rules
6. Support escalation process
7. Partner commercial model
8. Customer handoff process

## First 90-Day Partner Plan

| Period | Activity | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Days 1–30 | Identify and qualify first partners | Shortlist ready |
| Days 31–60 | Enable partners and sign agreements | Active partners |
| Days 61–90 | Assign first opportunities | First partner-led pipeline |

## Partner Controls

```text
Partners may not access production data without approval.
Partners may not receive secrets or credentials.
Partners may not promise unsupported features.
Partners may not bypass SNAD governance.
Partners must follow customer data handling rules.
```
