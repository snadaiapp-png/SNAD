# Stage 26 — Market Execution Plan

## Purpose

This document defines the market execution plan for SNAD after the completion of the enterprise growth and regional scale readiness stages.

The plan converts strategic readiness into a disciplined market execution program with target segments, channels, campaigns, commercial motions, and measurable outcomes.

## Target Segments

SNAD will initially focus on the following customer categories:

1. Small and medium businesses requiring unified operations
2. Growing companies needing ERP/CRM/HRM/Accounting consolidation
3. Service businesses requiring workflow automation
4. Retail and commerce operators requiring POS/Ecommerce readiness
5. Regional companies requiring Arabic-first operational systems

## Ideal Customer Profile

The initial enterprise ICP should meet at least four of the following:

- 20+ employees
- Multi-department operations
- Manual approval processes
- Separate CRM/accounting/HR systems
- Arabic and English operational needs
- Management reporting pain
- Willingness to run pilot implementation
- Executive sponsor available

## Market Motions

### 1. Direct Enterprise Sales

Used for qualified accounts that require consultative selling, pilot design, and executive engagement.

### 2. Partner-Led Implementation

Used when implementation partners can accelerate onboarding, configuration, and training.

### 3. Founder-Led Sales

Used for the first strategic customers where executive trust and product feedback are critical.

### 4. Content-Led Demand

Used to create awareness around Arabic-first BOS, AI-first operations, and workflow automation.

## Execution Phases

| Phase | Duration | Outcome |
|---|---:|---|
| Phase 1 | 0–30 days | Build target list, outreach scripts, pilot offer |
| Phase 2 | 31–60 days | Run demos, qualify accounts, start pilots |
| Phase 3 | 61–90 days | Convert first customers, activate partners |
| Phase 4 | 91–180 days | Repeatable commercial motion |

## Metrics

```text
Qualified leads

```

## Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Weak product-market fit | Use pilots and structured feedback |
| Long sales cycles | Focus on urgent workflow/accounting/operations pain |
| Implementation overload | Use partner-led onboarding |
| Legal/compliance gaps | Require legal/compliance checklist before enterprise closure |
