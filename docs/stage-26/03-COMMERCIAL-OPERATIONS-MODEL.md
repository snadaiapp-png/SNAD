# Stage 26 — Commercial Operations Model

## Purpose

This document defines how SNAD will operate commercially after production maturity. It establishes the operating model for pipeline management, pricing execution, quote-to-contract, billing handoff, customer onboarding, and revenue reporting.

## Commercial Operating Principles

```text
- Evidence-based sales decisions
- Clear pricing authority
- Controlled pilot-to-paid conversion
- No unsupported commercial promises
- Contract and compliance review before enterprise activation
- Revenue operations aligned with product and support readiness
```

## Commercial Process

### 1. Lead Qualification

Qualify leads using ICP fit, urgency, budget signal, implementation complexity, and compliance requirements.

### 2. Discovery

Capture business pain, current tools, operational workflows, number of users, entities, integrations, and localization needs.

### 3. Demo

Use a controlled demo flow focused on Arabic-first operations, workflow governance, tenant isolation, and post-production maturity.

### 4. Pilot Proposal

Define pilot scope, timeline, success metrics, support model, risks, exclusions, and conversion path.

### 5. Contract Review

Ensure ToS, MSA, DPA, SLA, privacy, and security terms are reviewed before enterprise commitments.

### 6. Onboarding Handoff

Transfer from sales to implementation/customer success using a documented handoff packet.

### 7. Billing Handoff

Move customer to billing operations only after pricing, subscription, taxation, and payment method readiness are documented.

## Roles

```text
Commercial Owner: owns pipeline and commercial decisions
Product Owner: confirms feature availability and roadmap fit
Implementation Owner: validates delivery effort
Customer Success Owner: owns activation and retention
Finance Owner: owns billing and revenue tracking
Legal/Compliance Owner: reviews contract and data obligations
```

## Revenue Operations Artifacts

```text
- Pipeline register
- Deal qualification sheet
- Pilot proposal template
- Pricing approval note
- Contract readiness checklist
- Customer onboarding handoff
- Billing activation checklist
- Renewal and expansion tracker
```

## Pricing Governance

```text
Discounts require owner approval
Custom enterprise pricing requires documented scope
Free pilots require defined duration and conversion criteria
No lifetime plan is allowed without explicit owner approval
```

## Commercial KPIs

```text
Qualified pipeline value
Demo-to-pilot conversion
Pilot-to-paid conversion
Average sales cycle
Average implementation time
Monthly recurring revenue readiness
Churn risk indicator
Expansion opportunities
```
