# Stage 26 — Operating Company Launch Model

## Purpose

This document defines the operating company launch model for SNAD Business Operating System | سند after the completion of Stages 20–25.

Stage 26 converts SNAD from a production-ready and enterprise-growth-ready platform into an operating company model capable of market execution, commercial accountability, partner operations, support delivery, revenue ownership, and regional readiness.

## Operating Company Objective

The operating company must own the following responsibilities:

1. Commercial execution
2. Enterprise customer acquisition
3. Partner activation
4. Support operations
5. Revenue operations
6. Compliance coordination
7. Product roadmap governance
8. Regional scale readiness

## Operating Model Pillars

### 1. Executive Ownership

The operating company requires a clearly assigned owner for:

- Market execution
- Customer contracts
- Partner program
- Revenue targets
- Support performance
- Risk acceptance
- Product prioritization

### 2. Functional Units

The initial operating company model contains the following units:

| Unit | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Executive Office | Strategy, governance, approvals, risk ownership |
| Product & Engineering | Product roadmap, releases, platform quality |
| Sales & Partnerships | Enterprise sales, partner recruitment, channel growth |
| Customer Success | Onboarding, adoption, retention, expansion |
| Support Operations | Incident response, SLA handling, customer support |
| Finance & Revenue Ops | Billing, subscriptions, reporting, collections |
| Legal & Compliance | Contracts, privacy, PDPL, security assurance |

## Launch Readiness Requirements

The operating company is not considered ready unless the following are documented:

- Sales ownership
- Customer success ownership
- Support ownership
- Billing ownership
- Partner ownership
- Compliance ownership
- Release governance ownership
- Incident escalation ownership
