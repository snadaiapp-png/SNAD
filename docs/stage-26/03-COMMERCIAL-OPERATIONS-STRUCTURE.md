# Stage 26 — Commercial Operations Structure

## Purpose

This document defines the commercial operations structure required to run SNAD as an operating company with accountable revenue execution, pipeline governance, partner motions, pricing discipline, and customer conversion tracking.

## Commercial Operating Principles

```text
Commercial execution must be measurable.
Revenue operations must be governed.
Pricing exceptions require approval.
Partner-sourced revenue must be tracked separately.
No customer promise may contradict production capability.
```

## Core Commercial Functions

| Function | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Sales Operations | Pipeline, demos, qualification, forecasting |
| Revenue Operations | Subscription lifecycle, billing readiness, reporting |
| Partner Operations | Partner recruitment, enablement, performance |
| Customer Success | Adoption, health scoring, retention, expansion |
| Product Feedback | Customer feedback into roadmap governance |

## Qualification Criteria

A customer cannot move to Pilot Proposed unless:

- Business pain is documented
- Executive sponsor is identified
- Target modules are defined
- Data/security requirements are known
- Implementation owner is assigned
- Pilot success criteria are measurable

## Revenue Operations Model

Revenue operations must track:

- Plan type
- Trial status
- Pilot status
- Subscription status
- Renewal date
- Expansion opportunity
- Churn risk
- Billing owner

## Partner Commercial Motion

Partners may support:

1. Implementation
2. Integration
3. Training
4. Localization
5. Sales referral
6. Marketplace extensions

Partner revenue must be classified as:

```text
Direct SNAD revenue
Partner referral revenue
Partner implementation revenue
Marketplace revenue
Professional services revenue
```

## Pricing Governance

Pricing exceptions require a documented approval record when:

- Discount exceeds approved threshold
- Customer requires custom commercial terms
- Pilot requires paid services
- Partner margin differs from standard model
- SLA commitments exceed standard support capacity
