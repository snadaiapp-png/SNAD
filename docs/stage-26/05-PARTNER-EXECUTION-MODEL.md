# Stage 26 — Partner Execution Model

## Purpose

This document defines the partner execution model for SNAD market launch. It turns the partner ecosystem and marketplace readiness from prior stages into an operating model for recruitment, qualification, activation, delivery, governance, and performance management.

## Partner Categories

```text
1. Implementation Partners
2. Integration Partners
3. Consulting Partners
4. Sales Partners
5. Marketplace Partners
```

## Partner Lifecycle

### 1. Identify

Source potential partners from ERP consultants, SaaS implementers, digital transformation firms, accounting consultants, and regional technology providers.

### 2. Qualify

Evaluate partner fit, market reach, technical capability, customer trust, operational maturity, and compliance posture.

### 3. Onboard

Provide partner orientation, product positioning, demo materials, implementation scope, governance rules, and support escalation paths.

### 4. Enable

Train partner teams on SNAD value proposition, onboarding flow, tenant setup, support boundaries, and implementation process.

### 5. Execute

Assign partner-supported opportunities, implementation tasks, and customer enablement responsibilities under SNAD governance.

### 6. Review

Measure partner-sourced pipeline, implementation quality, customer satisfaction, support burden, and compliance with rules.

## Partner Tiers

```text
Registered Partner: accepted into the program
Certified Partner: completed enablement and delivery validation
Premier Partner: proven delivery record and strategic account capability
```

## Partner Governance Rules

```text
- Partners do not receive production admin authority by default.
- Partners must follow tenant isolation rules.
- Partners may not publish integrations without review.
- Partner access must be least-privilege and revocable.
- Partner customer promises must match approved product capabilities.
- Partner commercial terms must be documented.
```
