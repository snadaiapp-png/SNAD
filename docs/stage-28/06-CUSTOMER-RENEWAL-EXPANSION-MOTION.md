# Stage 28 — Customer Renewal & Expansion Motion

## Purpose

This document defines the customer renewal and expansion motion for first paid customer readiness.

Although Stage 28 focuses on first paid conversion, renewal and expansion must be designed early so customer success, product usage, and revenue operations can track long-term value.

## Renewal Motion

Renewal readiness begins when the customer becomes a paid conversion candidate.

Renewal tracking fields:

```text
Customer
Subscription start date
Renewal date
Customer success owner
Executive sponsor
Adoption score
Support trend
Expansion signals
Renewal risk
Next business review
```

## Expansion Motion

Expansion can come from:

- Additional users
- Additional departments
- Additional modules
- Workflow automation expansion
- Reporting/analytics needs
- Partner-led implementation expansion
- Support tier expansion

## Customer Success Checkpoints

```text
30-day activation review
60-day adoption review
90-day value review
Renewal readiness review
Expansion fit review
Executive business review
```

## Expansion Qualification Criteria

An expansion opportunity is qualified when:

- Core scope is adopted
- Customer value is confirmed
- No unresolved critical support blocker exists
- Sponsor confirms additional need
- Commercial owner validates feasibility
- Product scope is supported

## Renewal Risk Signals

| Signal | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Low usage | Customer success intervention |
| Open critical blockers | Escalation |
| Executive sponsor absent | Sponsor re-engagement |
| Support dissatisfaction | Support review |
| Unclear value | Business value workshop |
| Billing concerns | Commercial review |

## Expansion Risk Controls

```text
Do not promise unsupported modules.
Do not expand scope without commercial approval.
Do not grant partner production access without approval.
Do not activate billing changes without billing approval.
Do not make AI decisions without human confirmation.
```
