# Stage 29 — Renewal & Expansion Execution

## Purpose

This document defines execution controls for renewal and expansion activities during the controlled paid launch stage.

Stage 29 starts the revenue operations execution cycle, so renewal and expansion signals must be captured from the first paid customer lifecycle.

## Execution Scope

```text
Renewal timeline tracking
Customer health tracking
Expansion opportunity qualification
Commercial change approval
Billing change approval
Customer success business review
Support and adoption trend review
```

## Renewal Execution Model

```text
Paid subscription activated
Customer onboarding confirmed
Usage and support monitored
Value review completed
Renewal risk assessed
Renewal plan prepared
Renewal decision tracked
```

## Expansion Execution Model

Expansion can proceed only when:

```text
Current paid scope is stable
Customer value is confirmed
No critical unresolved blocker exists
Expansion scope is supported
Commercial owner approves
Billing owner approves pricing or billing change
Customer success owner confirms readiness
```

## Renewal and Expansion Signals

| Signal | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| High adoption | Expansion possible | Qualify expansion |
| Low adoption | Renewal risk | Customer success intervention |
| Multiple departments engaged | Expansion possible | Map stakeholders |
| Billing dispute | Renewal risk | Billing support escalation |
| Support backlog | Renewal risk | Technical escalation |
| Sponsor engagement | Renewal strength | Schedule business review |

## Execution Rules

```text
Do not expand paid scope without commercial approval.
Do not change subscription billing without billing approval.
Do not promise unsupported modules.
Do not grant partner production access without approval.
Do not claim AI automation decisions without human confirmation.
```
